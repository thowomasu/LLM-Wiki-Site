Manual

How to use this CALB website.

1. Start with the claim

Use the first two sections to see the claim boundary: the pulse bridge passes internally, but drive-cycle transfer and external validation are not solved.

What was built

The work turned a raw archive into a repeatable DRT experiment package.

  • Built a CALB manifest without extracting the full 6.96 GB archive.
  • Loaded HPPC and EIS MAT files directly from the ZIP by cell, protocol, and temperature.
  • Found long HPPC discharge pulse windows and matched them to EIS records by nearest OCV.
  • Fit separate EIS DRT and time-domain DRT curves with non-negative regularized fits.
  • Compared gamma-shape agreement on overlapping tau support.
  • Added batch sections, failure audits, weighting probes, target-variant probes, tau coverage audits, tau-constrained fits, and protocol replication.
The useful part is not the code existing. The useful part is that the pipeline produced negative results first, isolated the failure mode, then produced a replicated fix.

Failure mode

The time-domain fit was spending gamma outside the EIS tau window.

That means the default time-domain DRT could fit voltage while placing relaxation mass where the EIS target could not confirm it. Comparing those shapes was structurally unfair.

Final 10 C rule

10 C was solved by fixing candidate selection, not by cheating OCV.

Drive-cycle validation

Drive-cycle transfer is where the story gets worse.

Section 34 looked good against a baseline-only drift model. Then Section 36 used a stronger calibrated ECM-style baseline, and fixed HPPC-derived DRT lost. Section 37 found pulse SOC alignment is tight but drive-cycle SOC alignment is bad. Section 42 then showed only a tiny aligned drive-cycle subset survives.

What is missing

The weak spots are clear now.

  • The pulse-test bridge gate is solved internally, but only for this fresh-cell CALB grid.
  • Drive-cycle protocols are tested in Section 34 against baseline-only drift, then stress-tested in Section 36 against a stronger calibrated ECM-style baseline. Fixed DRT loses that stronger audit: median 150.9172295922953 mV versus best ECM 134.8626012268972 mV.
  • The combined EIS/TDM objective needs better scaling. Equal weighting is too crude.
  • OCV and SOC alignment are still rule-based voltage matching, not a full state estimator. Section 37 says pulse alignment is tight, but drive-cycle SOC alignment is bad.
  • Section 42 makes the drive-cycle problem sharper: only 16 of 198 rows are within 5 percent SOC delta, and DRT wins 0.0 against best ECM in that aligned subset.
  • No aging or SOH claim has been proven. The current archive is characterization-rich, but this pipeline has not been converted into an aging predictor.
  • NASA and CALCE have now been qualified as external candidates. They have not validated the CALB rule.
Roadmap

Next work should be disciplined, not random.

Core locked

Regression currently passes 49 of 49 checks. Keep this as the guardrail before changing the rule.

Run CALCE first

Use the 24 25 C PLN cell candidates. Freeze the join keys before modeling: cell identifier, temperature, storage period, and SOC.

Keep NASA second

NASA has same-cell EIS plus charge/discharge cycling. Useful robustness check, weak first validation target.

Rebuild combined fitting

Try scale-normalized residuals, adaptive EIS/TDM weights, and protocol-aware regularization. Keep separate tau-constrained as the baseline to beat.

The next professor-facing milestone is not another internal rescue plot. It is a frozen-rule CALCE run with ECM baselines and honest failure reporting.
Glossary

Acronyms and codes used on this site.

CodeMeaningWhy it matters here
CALBChina Aviation Lithium Battery, the manufacturer of the L148N58A cell.Names the prismatic lithium-ion cell family used in the dataset.
L148N58ALarge prismatic lithium-ion cell model with 58 Ah nominal capacity.The paper and Mendeley repository are for this exact cell type.
EISElectrochemical Impedance Spectroscopy.Frequency-domain impedance target used to fit or compare DRT curves.
DRTDistribution of Relaxation Times.Transforms impedance or pulse relaxation into a relaxation-time spectrum.
HPPCHybrid Pulse Power Characterization.Pulse test used as the time-domain source for DRT fitting.
SOCState of Charge.Battery charge level. Bad SOC alignment is why drive-cycle claims are weak.
SOHState of Health.Not validated here. The CALB local work is fresh-cell characterization, not ageing prediction.
OCVOpen Circuit Voltage.Voltage used to match HPPC pulse candidates with EIS records.
ECMEquivalent Circuit Model.Baseline model used in the drive-cycle audit.
PLNCALCE storage dataset cell identifier used in external qualification files.Join key for capacity, SOC, temperature, storage period, and EIS records in the CALCE candidate dataset.
UDDS, US06, WLTPStandard dynamic driving profiles.These are the dynamic cycle tests used to stress drive-cycle transfer.
Cycle numberCount or identifier of repeated battery test cycles.The local CALB work has EIS cycle IDs in the EIS slice, but it does not contain ageing-by-cycle capacity validation.
